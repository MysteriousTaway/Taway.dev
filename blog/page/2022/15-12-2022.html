The failure of modern education

15.12.2022

In this blog post I will share my opinion on how the “modern” education system does not reflect our rapidly changing and developing society and how being stuck in the dark ages of education can harm the development of skills present in young people.

Personal experience

I have never been a big fan of our education system. It has failed me in many ways such as making me hate everything that it teaches. Since elementary school I was forced to be a part of physical education and the teachers made me hate it so much. Being expected to enjoy activities that I don’t like and then have good grades in these subjects is ridiculous and that brings me to my first point.

Pressure to score high

Our families, teachers, friends and our society as a whole expect everyone to be straight A student or at worst get Bs. Anything below is seen as failure. This is very emotionally and physically damaging to us students. Staying up all night, filling our brain with useless information only to write it on a piece of paper and then forgetting it is not something I or anyone else would enjoy.

Studying is not learning

Have you ever noticed that the word “learning” got quickly replaced by the word “studying”? A lot of people think they mean the same but that’s not true at all. Yes they are similar in the short term but not in the long term. To learn something means (at least to me) to remember a piece of information that you will use in the future or are using right away. Studying is something like learning, but also not at all. It basically means to remember a piece of information, fill out a test and forgetting what you “learned”. In my opinion this is meaningless and the time preparing could be spent elsewhere which would be more beneficial to the student.

No room for passion and self-improvement

Unless you are someone who can remember everything said in your classes then you as most other students have to spend a lot of your free time studying just to pass your classes. This approach to education leaves you with no time to develop skills that you yourself are passionate about. Just imagine the world where students could improve in areas they are passionate about. In my opinion this would be heaven. I am just one of many people with this philosophy. The person that inspired me was Maria Montessori who created the philosophy of hands-on and real-world skills oriented approach to learning. Unfortunately the Montessori education system is not wide-spread as I personally would like.

The non-modernism of “modern” education

Education has been firstly made mandatory in the Holy Roman empire (Germany 1592) by the Protestant Duchy of Pfalz-Zweibrücken. Even back then it was one room with a lot of students and one teacher. As you could imagine it was used to teach children how to read and write. I admit these skills were and still are extremely necessary, and one can’t really live in a society without them. But did you notice that the idea of a school and how to teach children did not change in over 400 years? Back then people did not know anything about human psychology or how kids should learn. Why are we doing the same thing for 400 f#cking years when the society and the world we live in has changed so dramatically? School teaches us to shut up and do what we’re told without questioning it, but modern employers are looking for people who can communicate their ideas with others and can provide much more to the company other than physical labour. Okay not all employers, but white collar positions our parents and grandparents wants us to work at do.

A few words at the end

As you could see I am someone who would love to live in an utopia or at least in a world with much better education system. Well as always my ideas will be called “copium” by my peers who would rather do nothing and let our education system rot, instead of expressing that there’s something deeply wrong and proposing new ideas how to fix these issues.
