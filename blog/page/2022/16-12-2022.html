Nostalgia

The internet era of our childhoods is long gone. Back in the day most if not all websites looked simple and even our operating systems felt like home. Nowadays everything feels like an office building. Clean and efficiency-oriented design with simple shapes and color palettes is dominating the interwebs.

Simply modern assistant

Back in the day when you opened the file browser a friendly dog called Rover would accompany you on your journey to discover the file you’re looking for. Rover was one of two virtual assistants waiting for you in Windows XP. The other one was Clippit (Clippy) who would help you while you were editing files in Microsoft Office. Both assistants had a quite complex visual design compared to modern Cortana. Unfortunately design of virtual assistants reflects the prevalent design of its era.

Chaotic design of old web

Long time ago when Amazon was still selling books; websites looked so random. No design idea was off the table. Orange text on dark blue background, red text on lime green. Fifteen different fonts on one page, but most of it was written in comic sans anyways. Half of the page written in italic. It was truly the wild west of internet era, but each website had its own sense of personality. Nowadays all websites use the same fonts and design philosophies. Not only the corporate ones but also personal websites look like that. I tried to avoid this when I was designing this one, because I want this website to reflect myself as the creator and not how the corporate world wants me to present myself. I wish more people did this. We could bring back the good ol’ days.

Operating system of yesterday

As you might have noticed this page looks a little bit like Windows XP. I did this to remind you how good it was before and how different it is now. Even though this operating system was very insecure compared to its modern day counterpart it for many people holds a special place in their hearts. Fun fact about WinXP: You could download so called “Christmas fun pack” to completely overhaul your computer’s visuals by setting a red Christmas theme. This was much more easy to do on this version of Windows.

Happy nostalgia!


NOTE: yes I am aware that my style of writing is chaotic but I write what I’m thinking about.
